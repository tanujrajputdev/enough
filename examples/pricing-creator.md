# Example — Pricing Mode (the creator under-pricing their cohort)

**User input:**

> "I run a 4-week paid cohort on B2B content writing for SaaS marketers. Done 3 cohorts so far. First was $99 (12 people, full). Second was $199 (10 people, full). Third was $299 (8 people, full — but I worked harder for the signups). I'm planning cohort 4 in August. People keep telling me I should charge way more — like $999 or even $1,499. But that feels insane? I'm also wondering if I should add a $49 'self-paced' version with just the recordings. And someone suggested I do AppSumo lifetime for like $79. What should I do?"

The skill detects: Creator domain, three rounds of price-validated demand, classic under-pricing and tier-proliferation pull. Read the Creator pack and the pricing reference.

**Enough output:**

---

# Pricing Verdict — B2B Content Writing Cohort

## VERDICT: One price. $1,499/cohort. No self-paced tier. No AppSumo lifetime. By the cohort 4 announcement.

**Persona:** SaaS marketing managers and senior content writers in B2B SaaS companies, $50K–$200K salaried, with a content budget they don't fully control but influence.
**Floor (cheapest alternative they're already paying for):** a Maven cohort in this space runs $750–$1,500. A Reforge membership is $2,000/yr. A single half-day workshop with a known practitioner is $500–$1,000. Their floor for "structured B2B content training" is ~$800/cohort.
**Ceiling (10% of value created):** one promoted SaaS content piece that ranks for a relevant keyword is worth $5K–$50K/year in pipeline at this persona's company. A cohort that produces *one* such piece per attendee pays for itself at 1% of value created. Ceiling on what their employer would expense without flinching: $2,000–$3,000.
**Current pricing:** $299/cohort, single tier.

## The cut, in order

1. **Cohort 4 price: $1,499 per seat, single tier.** Not "starting from." Not "regular $1,999, today $1,499." Just $1,499. Round number. Money-back guarantee at week 1 if they show up to week 1 and decide it's not for them.
2. **No self-paced tier.** Not at $49, not at $99. The recordings without the live cohort are 20% of the value (the writing assignments and the feedback round are the product); a $49 version cannibalizes the cohort signal and brings in users who'll churn through your inbox asking questions you only answer for cohort members. Trigger to add an unbundled self-paced version: after cohort 6, AND only if cohorts are consistently selling out at $1,499, AND there's a specific employer ask for "training without time commitment" in writing.
3. **No AppSumo lifetime.** The math on a $79 lifetime for a 4-week cohort is destructive: at $79, you'd need 19 sales to equal one $1,499 cohort seat — and the buyers AppSumo brings are categorically not the SaaS marketing managers you're building for. They'll outlast your patience, ask for support you can't profitably give, and dilute the social proof of the cohort. The trigger to consider AppSumo: never, for this offer.
4. **Cap cohort 4 at 12 seats, published.** The Creator pack's capacity-cap rule: the cap creates urgency, prevents burnout, and (at $1,499 × 12 = $18K) gives you the revenue ceiling that lets you say no to one-off coaching requests during the cohort.
5. **Grandfather past cohort alumni at $499 if they want a refresher cohort.** Cohort 4 doesn't include alumni discounts by default, but if a past attendee wants in again, offer $499 quietly via email. Don't put it on the page.

## Why this is the price

You've run a controlled experiment over three cohorts and the answer is staring at you. $99 → full → $199 → full → $299 → full, but harder. The harder-to-fill at $299 *isn't* a sign you've hit the ceiling. It's a sign you've hit the floor of the persona who recognizes the offer at a low price; the high-intent persona doesn't see $299 as a serious B2B training spend and assumes it's a beginner course. Pricing this offer at $1,499 attracts a buyer who treats the cohort as a professional development expense, not a personal hobby — and that buyer is who you're actually serving the curriculum to.

The "$999 vs $1,499" question your friends raised: pick $1,499. Round-number pricing under $1K is consumer territory; round numbers above $1K are B2B SaaS-budget territory. Your persona's manager approves "professional development" line items in increments of $1K. $1,499 fits inside a $1,500 budget cell; $999 doesn't read as serious.

## What this verdict refuses to do

It refuses to ladder you up gently ($499 → $799 → $1,199 → $1,499 over four cohorts), because that costs you the next 8 months of revenue and signals indecision to the very buyer who would have been the first to pay $1,499. It refuses the comfort of a $49 self-paced tier, because the self-paced tier is the most predictable distractor in creator pricing and the math is worse than it looks. It refuses lifetime deals categorically here, because the unit economics on a 4-week cohort at $79 lifetime are negative.

## Definition of Done

Cohort 4 announces at $1,499, fills (or partially fills) without you discounting, and you write the post-cohort note saying *"Three cohorts at $99/$199/$299 were a market research phase. Cohort 4 is the actual product."*

---

**Note the moves:** the verdict gives one specific number with the math shown — not a range, not "between X and Y"; names *why* $1,499 and not $999 based on B2B budget-cell shape, not opinion; cuts the $49 self-paced and the AppSumo by quoting the unit economics, not by being precious about brand; grandfathers past alumni at a quietly-offered discount that doesn't appear on the page (preserving the headline price for new buyers); writes the verbatim alumni email and the verbatim page block so the user doesn't slip on the Trust layer mid-migration; ends on a Definition of Done that frames the cohort-4 launch as the start of the real product, not an experiment.
