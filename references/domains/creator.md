# Domain Pack — Creator / Content

Apply this pack when the persona is a creator, writer, newsletter operator, course seller, info-product builder, indie consultant productizing their expertise, or anyone whose product is fundamentally *their own knowledge or audience*. Detect from the brief: newsletter, course, info-product, community, paid Substack, cohort, audience monetization, "build in public," "I have X followers and I want to…"

The creator overbuild instinct is *platform theater* — building like the product needs to be a SaaS before the underlying expertise has been sold once. Cut accordingly.

## The persona truths that change cuts

- **The audience IS the product, pre-PMF.** Creators sell their judgment, taste, or expertise. The first version is rarely software; it's a paid email, a one-off cohort, a paid Slack, a paid call. Software is what gets built once the offer has proven it sells.
- **Distribution is mostly already solved.** The creator has a platform (newsletter, X, LinkedIn, YouTube). Building distribution into the product is usually a cut.
- **Burnout is the silent killer.** Creator products fail at month 4–6 because the creator is the bottleneck on fulfillment. Concierge mode is genuinely sustainable only with a cap. Build the cap in.

## Cut tests, recalibrated for Creator

1. **Loop test — start with no product.** The smallest test is often a tweet that says "I'm running a paid cohort on X for 8 people, ₹15K each, replies open." If 8 strangers reply, the loop worked before a single page was built. Defer any software until the offer is proven by money.
2. **Walk-away test — measured against the creator's reputation.** The product walks away the moment the creator's name is no longer worth what's charged. Trust layer is heavier here: every shipped piece is a referendum on the creator.
3. **Concierge test — extra weight, in two directions.** Concierge fulfillment is *the* play for creator products; manual is the V1 for most cohorts, audits, consults. BUT — concierge with no cap is how creators burn out. Set the cap before shipping. ("Max 10 students per cohort; max 4 audits per month.")
4. **Spreadsheet test — applies to almost every "platform feature."** A creator's first community is a WhatsApp group, not a Circle/Discourse install. Their first course is a Google Doc with Loom embeds, not Teachable. Their first audit is a hand-built PDF, not a dashboard.

## Overbuild patterns specific to Creator

In addition to the general `cut-patterns.md`:

### Custom course platform / LMS at launch
- **Why:** "Teachable takes 5%."
- **Fails:** spreadsheet test; pre-PMF, the LMS is a Google Doc + Loom + Stripe link. Save 5% AFTER ₹5L MRR.
- **Ship instead:** Notion or Google Doc curriculum + Loom or Riverside videos + Stripe/Razorpay link + a WhatsApp group. Trigger to platform: 100+ paid students AND the operations cost of the manual stack exceeds the platform's fee.

### Custom community platform (Circle / Discourse / Mighty Networks clone)
- **Why:** "we want a real community feel."
- **Fails:** loop test; the community happens in the user's existing apps, not on a new tab they have to remember.
- **Ship instead:** WhatsApp or Slack or Discord, whichever the persona already lives in. Trigger to platform: the existing channel breaks under volume (≥1,000 active members), with specific complaints.

### Custom membership site / paywalled blog
- **Why:** "Substack takes 10%."
- **Fails:** sequencing; the audience comes first, the publishing platform after.
- **Ship instead:** paid Substack/Beehiiv/ConvertKit. Trigger to migrate: revenue ≥ ₹5L/mo AND a specific feature the host doesn't have is blocking growth.

### "Build my audience first then sell" — the content engine without an offer
- **Why:** safer to publish than to charge.
- **Fails:** cut-pattern #18; without an offer to compound toward, content is unfocused.
- **Ship instead:** the offer first, even at zero audience. A landing page with a price and a "join the waitlist with a deposit" link. Then content that compounds toward the offer.

### Sponsorship management software / brand-deal dashboard
- **Why:** "creators need this."
- **Fails:** concierge test; the creator has 4 sponsors a year, not 40 a month.
- **Ship instead:** a spreadsheet. Trigger to platform: ≥30 sponsorship inquiries/month, named.

### Custom analytics dashboard for newsletter / channel performance
- **Why:** existing tools "don't show the right metric."
- **Fails:** ego test / one-more-feature; the right metric is usually one number the existing tool shows.
- **Ship instead:** a weekly note-to-self with the 2–3 numbers that move the offer. Trigger to platform: the creator's *students* ask for it, not the creator.

### Course-builder features before any student has finished a course
- **Why:** anticipating future students.
- **Fails:** concierge test; the first 10 students get their experience hand-built and the creator learns what the platform should do.
- **Ship instead:** the curriculum lives in a Notion or Doc; assignments are emailed; grading is a Loom feedback video. Trigger to platform: ≥3 cohorts run AND the manual stack breaks somewhere specific.

### Mobile app for the audience to "consume content on the go"
- **Why:** "creators have apps."
- **Fails:** cut-patterns #8 + the audience already has apps (Spotify, YouTube, the newsletter inbox).
- **Ship instead:** never, unless the product is itself an app concept. Otherwise: distribute through the apps the audience already uses.

## The One Metric for Creator

Pre-PMF: *N paying strangers in your target persona who bought the offer and (where applicable) showed up / completed / referred someone*. Sales alone aren't enough — completion, repeat, or referral proves the offer was worth what was charged.

Kill Criteria default: *if fewer than 5 strangers (not friends, not warm intros) pay within 30 days of the offer going live, the offer is wrong, not the funnel.*

## The Trust layer for Creator

- **The creator's name is on it.** Promises in the product copy must match what the creator has said elsewhere — newsletters, X, podcasts. Inconsistency is the dishonesty users punish hardest.
- **The capacity cap is published.** "Max 10 in this cohort. Doors close on [date]." Caps create urgency AND prevent the burnout that kills creator products.
- **Refunds for cohorts/courses follow the value-delivered rule.** Pro-rated on cohorts; no-questions-asked for a window on info-products; never silent.
- **The community channel survives the launch.** A creator who launches a paid Slack and then disappears from it has betrayed the offer. If the creator can't show up, the offer was wrong; cut it.

## What an Enough Spec looks like in this domain

A Creator Enough Spec almost always reads:

- **CORE:** the offer (cohort / audit / consult / paid newsletter / info-product) — described in one paragraph the persona will recognize.
- **TRUST:** Stripe/Razorpay link, capacity cap, refund policy, the creator's promise written in the creator's voice.
- **LATER:** custom platform, mobile app, analytics, sponsorship dashboard, multi-cohort scheduling, automated grading.
- **NEVER (for now):** LMS clone, community platform clone, paid app, brand-deal dashboard, gamification.

If the user's spec leads with "the LMS / the community platform / the app" before the offer has sold once — the deepest cut is *don't build software, sell the offer first*. Make it gently; this cut saves months.
